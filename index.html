I'm Hanh-Linh (Linh) Tran, a PhD Candidate in Political Science at Emory University. My research lies at the intersection of political violence, human rights, and religion. My dissertation examines how experiences of and resistance to repression shape long-term protest patterns among Vietnamese Catholics. My other research encompasses post-war migration, autonomous regions, and practices of human rights as well as public opinion toward human rights. Geographically, my work focuses on Southeast Asia and Eastern Europe, which share legacies of communism and religious persecution. My research has been supported by J-PAL's Governance Initiative, the SEAREG Pre-Dissertation Fellowship, and the Institute for Humane Studies.

I am a co-organizer of the Junior Scholars in Quantitative Conflict Workshop and a member of the South China Sea Data Initiative.

I hold an MA in Political Science from Central European University and a BA in English for International Relations from the Diplomatic Academy of Vietnam.

You can reach me at hanh.linh.tran@emory.edu.

A note on my name: Although my full first name is Hanh-Linh, it can be difficult to pronounce. Please feel free to call me Linh, pronounced as either /lin/ or /liɲ/.
