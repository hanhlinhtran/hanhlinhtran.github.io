Before graduate school, I was a part-time artists/illustrator in Hanoi. My work captures my life–coffee, cats, plants, landscapes, and figure drawings. I use bold and vivid colors, reflecting my profound affection for life and the intense emotions I harbor towards both people and objects. While I find joy in depicting landscapes and figures, my style is not realism. Rather than replicating how objects actually appear, my goal is to convey how I look at them. My color patches are not static; they pulse with dynamic threads, mirroring my perception of the world. Objects, to me, are in perpetual motion internally, always evolving, their mere static presence hinting at imminent changes. In a paradoxical dance, my color technique strives to freeze a moment as a never-changing memory, yet simultaneously revealing the ever-changing nature inherent in people and objects. Put it simply, I try to capture the dynamics within staticity.

My artwork has appeared in the Forth and Fifth Hanoi Open Exhibitions (2017 & 2018). I and Eriko Hamada showcased our joint project Mi going to Japan whose poster can still be found at Tranquil Books & Coffee 15B Tran Hung Dao, Hanoi. Recently my work was showcased at B. Jones Gallery (Emory University) in Fall 2024.

Although graduate studies has got me busy, I always find time to create original artwork. To keep my passion alive, I sell merches--postcards, stickers, zines, and keychains--from my artwork in minifairs whenever I am back to my homecountry. My products can be found at Quest Bookstore (Hanoi) and Da Nhan (Phu Yen).
